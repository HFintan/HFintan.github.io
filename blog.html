November 10th

Today we went to CADBURYWORLD! The neighbourhood the dynasty had built for its workers is lovely. There's a delicious smell of chocolate wafting through the air outside. Then we went on the tour through the factory. Seems some European guy was given a Ghanian slave girl and she told him stories of chocolate and some God, and the European then convinced the leader of the Ghanian tribe that he was the God in question, presumably to the end of stealing the secrets of chocolate. Then there were some informative holograms and wobbly benches where Mr. Cadbury's told how he "even employed a costumed Chinaman to work in his shop" as the industry burgeoned under the caring eyes of the Cadbury family who seemed to care only for the welfare of their workers. They fed us with chocolate (including something that tasted like glue with white Buttons in it) so that when we finally made it to the largest Cadbury's shop in the world, we weren't really inclined to buy anything. Still did, mind, but not too much.
We also went for Chinese takeaway. They didn't have chopsticks, but aside from that it was pretty good.

November 9th

Spent most of today in bed, with the best, but unfulfilled hopes of doing some work. Eventually got up and wandered to Harborne with Mark where we saw OWLS! The birds of prey sanctuary had a few of them out to raise money, but had packed most of them away since the time Mark had spotted them in his earlier wanderings. I was chuffed that I got to see a long-eared owl (I think), a snow owl, a little owl and a common kestrel though. The latter was fecking tiny. Then we explored some charity shops and I bought some cheap (and so far crap) DVDs. Did cook a successful dinner of steak, beans and rice though.

November 8th

Got up at stupidly early o'clock, caught the train to London, spent most of the trip reading a maths paper, spent an hour wandering around to find the JobCentre, did the interview and then had to kill about eight hours before my train home. I bought credit in the O2 store. That was more exciting than it ought to have been, as the lass working there took my number and then sent me a confirmation text to check it was the right one, which I duly received. Bear with me - instead of giving me a top-up credit piece of paper, what she actually did was to send the credit directly to my phone, which I thought was pretty inefficient, as it saves one having to listen to all the tosh about how "O2 have now made it even easier to top up - now listen while I list all of the stupid ways you could top up, before finally allowing you to top up the way that you want to". However, and here my tale becomes a little non-chronological for I wandered off at this point, I later discovered that the credit had been sent to the wrong phone number. So I had to go back and complain and your one said that she had sent it to the number I had given her, but fortunately I was able to point out that the number I had given her had a text from her and was different to the number on the receipt. She said I'd left it too long to complain, so I had to - for the first time ever - "ask to speak to the manager". I didn't like doing it, but got it sorted in the end. This, combined with the absence of the SIM from the phone when I bought it, and the ridiculous prices over here, lead me, a loyal O2 customer for the past six years or so, to say "Fuck you, O2."
I went for a hot towel shave, which wasn't really the same relaxing experience as with that gent down at the Bridge Mills in Galway. The lack of general hygiene as I bled all over the hot towel and tissue paper was less of a concern than the lunatic customer behind me - a friend of the barbers' - ranting on in unintelligible Brummiese about various life issues he was having and how he was going to deal with those who were allegedly responsible. There was also a woman who came in and sat behind me for a while, next to the chair where I'd left my jacket and rucksack, and then left without much more than a farewell to the barbers. I was trying to roll my eyes around in my head to see whether she were attempting to pilfer what little I had in the bag, but couldn't comfortably turn my head with the razor poised at my throat. It was probably only paranoia and an overactive imagination which made me concerned that the barbers regularly robbed their customers at razor-point. Fleet Street was over two miles away. I survived anyway, not completely unscathed, but at least the bits of skin left hanging off my face didn't have hair on them.
Then I wandered around for over an hour trying to find the train station, only to accidentally take shelter in it when it started to rain. Having thus found my bearings, I searched for something to do, but it was too miserably wet to go much further than the British Library, which is worth seeing if you, for some reason, find yourself in London. Loads of really old books 'n shit. I also bought three books while waiting there for the rain to stop - Kurt Vonnegut, George Bernard Shaw, and Jose Saramago. Cultureful or what?
Then I wandered into another keepsake from a bygone era - an internet café. I played chess online there for about an hour, then wandered back to the train station for a quick bite to eat and an hour or two spent watching the people rushing past to catch their trains.
London is boring. Don't go.

November 7th

Well, I spent almost the entirety of today writing an algorithm. It is useless, I think, but it was more fun than reading relevant research papers. The idea is that you have an equation Ax=b where A is an m by n matrix, b is a vector of length n, and x is a vector of length m, which has k non-zero entries which have integer values between 1 and t. The algorithm takes, as input, the matrix A, the sparsity k and the upper bound for the integers t. It then calculates all possible bs given those constraints. Actually, I spent today generalising the algorithm - I had most of it done yesterday evening for the case k=2. I enjoyed it though, which is the important thing. That was the height of today's excitement. I also booked train tickets for tomorrow's trip to London. The cheapest I could get cost about £72 return which is a bit fucking ridiculous. From now on, I'll be taking the bus. It doesn't look like the two friends I had hoped to meet tomorrow will be able to meet up - one is recording and the other has an "infected graze". So I should have twelve hours' worth of London adventures to relate tomorrow. How exciting. Oh, also, it seems that Mark left the front door unlocked for most of today and we weren't burgled! Yay! I don't feel comfortable ending this post on a high note though, so... another thing which bugs me about Birmingham are the pedestrian crossings. I hate when there's one car driving along the road and they feel obliged to wave you across. It doesn't do anyone any good.

November 6th

Imagine I have painted an image which comprises a line of four pixels. You know that there are only 256 possible values for each of these pixels. Further, assume that you know that only two of the pixels are coloured in. Imagine the pixels are very heavy and I can afford only one carrier pigeon. You might think that to reconstruct the image you would need to know both the values of the two coloured-in pixels and their positions in the row of pixels. However, using the matrix

 
 
-61 -5 191 -379
-233 401 101 29
 
 

you can recover the original masterpiece from a mere two values! That matrix isn't unique, but it took me almost as long to find as it did to try and construct it in html.
In other news, Mark arrived, we went to the shop, got soaked, played chess, cooked two pizzas because they were too big to fit in the freezer... it was all terribly exciting. I also had coconut milk, which is stupidly sweet and not at all as nice as one might have imagined.

November 4th

Bought some stuff for when the horde of people wanting to visit me start showing up. Bedlinen, pots, pans, an extra plate and so on... Then I went to the office for a while and did some trying to understand stuff. Then I went home and did some cooking with my fancy new pots, largely at the behest of people who insisted that I shouldn't be eating sandwiches and pasties all of the time. I neglected to soak the pots first, which, apparently, you're supposed to do. I put on far too much rice, but fortunately half of it stuck to the non-stick saucepan which saved me having to eat it. And the rice I could detach from the pot was undercooked and a little crunchy. Are there consequences to eating undercooked rice? Yes, the general consensus seems to be, if, like a pigeon, you eat too much and your stomach swells up and bursts and you die. I know - I consulted the internet. "It's not unhealthy, but the rice will swell in your stomach. If you eat a large amount, you could certainly have discomfort. In extreme cases, your stomach could even burst." Not unhealthy, eh?
Eating uncooked rice can also increase your menstrual flow.
I had a tin of beans and, for meat, a packet of Findus crispy pancakes. The latter are succulent gravy and meat filling wrapped in cardboard, and chef Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall apparently swears by them. I must admit, I swore a little too. Tune in tomorrow, for more delicious recipes à la moi, or to see if I have avoided an exploding stomach and menstrual bleeding.

November 2nd

Lazy start to the day, but eventually went for a walk to find the nearest guitar shop. Many, many guitars, lots of kids playing electric guitars so loud I could barely hear what I was playing myself, and so many staff waiting to pounce when one made a decision that the entire process was quite uncomfortable. Eventually picked out a Baby Taylor for about £300 including various bits, then spent the whole walk home regretting having been subtly pressurised into making a snap decision (after about an hour of trying a selection of guitars in the shop) but then when I got in and played the guitar a bit, I was very happy with my purchase. Yay! Also, on my walk home with the guitar I passed a guy and his two daughters heading in the same direction, lugging a pallet. I offered him a hand, and during the course of the stroll it transpired that he, too, played guitar, and he invited me to come and join himself and a couple of people he jams with. He took my email address and promised to send me a list of the stuff that they play. We shall see what becomes of that. The healthiest thing I had to eat today was two bowls of Alpen. That stuff is about 37% sugar. Ridiculous.

November 1st

Met the supervisor today. I can't tell whether he thinks I'm an idiot or not yet. He took my phone number and promised that if the weather is fine in the summer we can go for a drive somewhere nice outside the city. I'm not entirely sure whether he was being serious or not. My previous supervisor emailed me yesterday to ask how I was getting on over here - that, I felt was an appropriate level of nice. One of the other guys in the office has a row of chilli plants growing on the windowsill and the other sees fit to have his private phone conversations in the office simply because they're in French. No, I've not managed to glean anything terribly juicy yet. I used my debit card for the first time today. Now I'm terrified that I'll end up bankrupt, whether by someone stealing all my details, or by me getting carried away with the ease of this online purchasing lark. On the upside, I seem to have been paid by the university for the work I have allegedly done so far. Yay! I've not got a useful context, but given the day that's in it, the phrase "Now is the winter of our discothéque..." has formulated itself in my head and will not go away.
