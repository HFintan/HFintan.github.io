Research Interests
My doctoral thesis focused on developing some theory and software for efficiently computing the homology of Euclidean data sets in up to four dimensions. This software was an extension of work done by Graham Ellis as part of Hap, a homological algebra library for use with the GAP computer algebra system. This extension exploited certain mathematical advantages of using permutahedrally-based structures, rather than traditional simplicial or cubical ones, to speed up and expand the scope of certain implementations of homological image computations.
The software should be available here soon, along with the relevant manual, tutorial and libraries.

The original, lofty ambition of the software was to analyse medical images, specifically scans of the prostate. Theoretically, the software and methods could be useful for efficient, automated detection, identification and monitoring of cancerous areas of the prostate. In practice, however, the medical data proved impossible to analyse, with some further work on the image segmentation needed. At the moment, I am researching shape reconstruction from shading to investigate the possibility of improving the software's recognition capabilities, with an eye, if you'll excuse the pun, to detecting macular degeneration and similar optical defects.
